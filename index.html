Pick Your Poison!

You finish setting up your campaign offices and organizing your supporters. Two lobbies with opposing interests approach you, looking for a candidate to back.

Do you throw your lot in with the plucky environmentalists or the well-connected industrialists?

Your choice to court the support of the environmentalists has a good effect on your reputation and a small positive effect on your funds as well.

Your choice to court the support of the industrialists increases your popularity among the working class. Their well-organized interest groups make a substantial contribution to your campaign.

Policies Schmolicies!

You receive an opportunity to speak publicly about your campaign policies. However, 'Boring News Tonight' only offers you limited time to speak about your tax policies.

How do you use this precious airtime?

Your decision to cut taxes across the board increases your reputation, because deep down, nobody wants to pay taxes. Your financial backers make additional contributions to your campaign.

Your declaration causes the middle and lower classes to flock to your banner, giving you a significant increase in reputation. However, some of your more wealthy supporters jump ship.

Your railing against government spending, while not directly answering the question, garners financial support from new special interest groups.

Curse Your Cursive!

The general election is rapidly approaching and you begin to feel the pressure. Unfortunately, your opponent runs attack ads criticizing the handwriting you demonstrated in your third grade cursive class.

How do you respond to this egregious slight?

Your attack ads successfully draw attention away from your poor penmanship, but they deal a blow to your campaign coffers.

While ethically commendable, your decision to take the high road does nothing to combat the negative press.

You decide to publicly shame your opponent's tactics, casting yourself as more honest than your opponent. In this effort you are successful. However, the advertising push is a substantial drain on funding.

Tweet!

In a recent tweet, the incumbent calls you a drunk, and claims you would "empty the country's coffers like you have emptied so many bottles."

How do you handle the situation?

Your response to the president is no surprise to anyone. You really are a typical politician.

Your snark is lauded by your base, but the general population now views you as a condescending elitist.

Your lack of a response provokes the President to eat his phone. He suffocates, and the Veep takes the helm. You are that much closer to the Oval.

Hacked!

Your Facebook account is hacked, and all of your private messages are made public. Among these messages are some rather "revealing" photographs of yourself.

How do you respond?

Your austerity helps stave off any uncomfortable questions, while the press generated by the controversy increases your name recognition.

Your fervent calls for "an end to the wild-west of cyberspace" rouses support from voters threatened by technology. The largest national cybersecurity firm, 'TechnoSecuritizmo Inc.,' increases their funding contributions accordingly.

You strike gold! Your opponent immediately releases financial records, revealing a slew of insider-trading offenses.

What do you do?

Nicely done. You are not afraid of a bit of confrontation. Your confidence under pressure inspires the public.

I'm not saying it's aliens, but... it's aliens!

Irrefutable proof of intelligent extraterrestrial life is revealed to the public. Alien vessels gently bob off the coastline of California. Tourists flock to have their pictures taken with the soggy saucers.

How do you adapt to this 'Alien' challenge?

Your show of bravado earns funding from organizations within the military industrial complex and support from the more trigger happy members of your constituency.

Your accusations make front page news, but not for the reasons you intended. The incumbent makes a statement that "if we had been covering up aliens for years and you didn't know about it... Well, that wouldn't make me the foolish one."

Your antics with our new friends warms the hearts of millions, after you explain the concept of a 'keg stand' to an alien. Nobody questions your diplomacy skills now! However, the party isn't free. You lose the backing of a major utility, as the alien's technology render the company's coal-fired plants obsolete.

Butt of the Joke!

How do you react?

Sulking does not pay off. The Daily Show continues making jokes at your expense, and ratings rise as a result.

Self-awareness suits you. Your renewed sense-of-self empowers you to lead more effectively.

You successfully hide your disingenuous nature. The younger voters find you charismatic. Who doesn't?
